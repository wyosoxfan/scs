SOUTHWEST COUNSELING SERVICE HISTORY

Southwest Counseling Service is a comprehensive, community mental health and substance abuse treatment center in Sweetwater County with offices located in Rock Springs and Green River, Wyoming. Southwest Counseling Service was originally named Sweetwater County Counseling and was founded in 1962. The name was changed in 1965 and the center has been a part of Sweetwater County since. The agency originally employed five workers and since then has grown to over 140 employees.


Accomplishments

Southwest Counseling Service has been a leader in the State of Wyoming for behavioral health.

Accomplishments include:

  • First to employ a psychiatric advanced practice registered nurse with prescriptive privileges in 1986
  • In 1989, Southwest Counseling Service opened the first psychosocial group home in the state
    • Also, developed and implemented a semi-independent living program for individuals in need of less-intense care
  • In 2000, Southwest Counseling Service opened the first therapeutic community residential substance abuse treatment program
  • Opened the first 28-day substance abuse treatment program in the state with Rosen Recovery Center in 1988
    • Now a short-term substance abuse residential treatment program
  • Opened the first women’s addiction program in the state in 2000
    • Was the first residential treatment program in the state to allow women to bring their children into treatment with them
  • The first community mental health and substance abuse treatment center in Wyoming to be Commission on Accreditation of Rehabilitation Facilities (CARF) accredited in 2001 and has since been accredited
 
WAMHSAC
 

Southwest Counseling Service is a part of the Wyoming Association of Mental Health and Substance Abuse Centers (WAMHSAC).

The WAMHSAC is made up of the mental health and substance abuse centers throughout the state of Wyoming that serve as the delivery system for state funded public mental health and substance abuse treatment.

Additional Information
  • All centers have a volunteer governing board.
  • All WAMHSAC participants are accredited and meet national standards regarding treatment services.
  • Services are designed to meet the needs of each center’s community. Client involvement and input are key to the development of these services.
  • The WAMHSAC is comprised of 20 centers throughout Wyoming that provide outpatient and/or residential mental health and substance abuse prevention and treatment services.
  • WAMHSAC centers provide a comprehensive array of services, making it possible for clients to access all mental health and substance abuse treatment needs. This approach reduces duplication, produces collaborative efforts, and provides the best opportunity for a seamless system of care, which means better care for you and your loved ones.
  • WAMHSAC centers provide sliding fees and accept most insurance, to help make treatment affordable
